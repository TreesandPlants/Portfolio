About

Weather forecasting or weather prediction is the application of science and technology to predict the conditions of the atmosphere for a given location and time.

More to Know

Origin

In 650 BC, the Babylonians predicted the weather from cloud patterns as well as astrology. In about 350 BC, Aristotle described weather patterns in Meteorologica. Later, Theophrastus compiled a book on weather forecasting, called the Book of Signs. Chinese weather prediction lore extends at least as far back as 300 BC, which was also around the same time ancient Indian astronomers developed weather-prediction methods.

Communication

A weather presenter (also known as a weather girl, weatherman or weather broadcaster) is a person who presents the weather forecast daily on radio, television or internet news broadcasts.

More to Know

Evolution

Weather forecasting has evolved from ancient observations and folklore to sophisticated, computer-driven models. Early methods relied on patterns in nature, astrology, and even cloud formations, while the modern era is characterized by scientific understanding of atmospheric physics, numerical prediction, and advanced technologies.
